# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Home buyers, refinancers, and real estate investors across Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Louisiana, Michigan, and Tennessee. They arrive with a specific goal (buy a first home, pull cash out, use a VA benefit, refinance to lower their payment) and are comparison-shopping lenders. Many are mid-funnel: they've done some research but haven't committed. They need fast answers, clear program comparisons, and a lender they can trust to actually close.

Secondary audience: repeat buyers, self-employed borrowers, and investors who have hit walls at banks and need a broker who can access more programs.

## Product Purpose

American Mortgage Services (brand: We Get It Closed) is a wholesale mortgage broker with 40+ years combined experience. The site's job is to:

1. Build fast trust with real signals (4.8-star Google rating, named loan officers, verified closings)
2. Route borrowers to the right loan program by intent, not by forcing them to know mortgage jargon first
3. Explain each loan program clearly so borrowers can self-qualify their path before calling
4. Make it easy to contact or apply, with zero friction at the final step

Success: a borrower lands on a state page or loan program page, understands their options in under 2 minutes, and submits a contact form or calls (888) 838-9330.

## Brand Personality

Trusted, Direct, Fast.

Voice: confident but not arrogant. Speaks plainly about how mortgages work. Uses "you" and "your scenario" not "our clients." Never hypes. Never overpromises. Earns credibility through clarity, not claims.

Emotional goal: borrowers should feel like they found the loan officer who actually knows what they're doing, not a bank portal or a lead-gen machine.

## Anti-references

- **Generic mortgage template sites**: stock photo couples in front of houses, clip-art key icons, blue/green gradient heroes, "We're Here to Help" copy that says nothing.
- **Big bank sterile** (Chase, Wells Fargo digital): corporate, cold, bureaucratic. Feels like a waiting room. Zero personality.
- **Old-school broker sites**: cluttered text walls, outdated CMS layouts, 2010-era design, illegible on mobile.
- **Rocket Mortgage flashy**: overly animated, tech-startup energy, neon accents, "pre-approve in 30 seconds" vibe. We close deals, we don't gamify them.

## Design Principles

1. **Route before you explain.** Borrowers arrive with a goal, not a loan type. Surface the path (intent-routing cards) before the explanation. Don't make them read to find where they belong.
2. **Earned authority, not borrowed.** Every trust signal must be real: named reviews, actual star rating, specific loan officer names, real closing stories. No fake badges, fake seals, or inflated claims.
3. **Compliance is the floor, not the ceiling.** Safe language (may qualify, subject to guidelines, talk with a loan officer) is non-negotiable — but it can still be specific, helpful, and persuasive. Constraint forces precision.
4. **One site, six states.** Shared loan program content, distinct state context and personality. Never thin duplication; always something worth landing on.
5. **Speed is credibility.** Fast page load = trustworthy lender. Every image, script, and component earns its weight.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Mobile-first responsive design. Forms must be keyboard-navigable with visible focus states. Color contrast ratios must pass for both body text and CTA buttons. Reduce motion via `prefers-reduced-motion` on all transitions. No icons-only affordances without labels.
