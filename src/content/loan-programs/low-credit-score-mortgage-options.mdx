## Mortgages for Lower Credit Scores

A lower credit score doesn't necessarily mean homeownership is out of reach, but it does narrow your program options and may affect the terms available to you. The right path depends on your full financial picture: your income stability, down payment amount, employment history, and the overall pattern of your credit.

## What Credit Score Is "Low" for a Mortgage?

Credit score thresholds vary by program:

- **Below 500:** Very limited options; most programs require at least 500
- **500-579:** FHA with 10% down payment is one of the few available options
- **580-619:** FHA (3.5% down), certain VA and USDA programs, some specialty lenders
- **620-639:** FHA, VA, USDA, and some conventional programs
- **640+:** Broader access across programs; better rate options

## FHA Loans for Lower Credit Scores

FHA is often the most accessible path for borrowers with credit scores below 640. Key parameters:

- Scores of 580 or higher: 3.5% minimum down payment
- Scores of 500 to 579: 10% minimum down payment
- Lender overlays may require higher scores than FHA minimums

FHA looks at your full credit picture, not just the score. Late payments in the past 12 months, open collections, and recent derogatory items can all affect approval even if your score meets the minimum.

## VA Loans for Eligible Veterans

VA loans don't have a government-mandated minimum credit score, but individual lenders apply their own overlays. Many VA lenders will work with scores starting at 580-620. If you're a veteran with a lower credit score, VA may offer a path that conventional or FHA doesn't.

## Compensating Factors Matter

A lower credit score can sometimes be offset by compensating factors, including:

- Larger down payment (reduces lender risk)
- Substantial liquid reserves (several months of payments in the bank)
- Low debt-to-income ratio (strong income relative to obligations)
- Stable employment history
- On-time rental history (even if not on credit report)

No single compensating factor is a guarantee, but a strong overall profile can sometimes support approval even with a lower score.

## Credit Repair as a Path

If your credit score is too low for the programs you're targeting, it may be worth taking time to improve it before applying. Common credit improvement strategies include:

- Paying down credit card balances below 30% utilization
- Disputing inaccurate negative items on your credit report
- Establishing positive payment history on existing accounts
- Avoiding opening new credit accounts before applying

Talk with a loan officer about your timeline. In some cases, a modest score improvement can open meaningfully better program options.
