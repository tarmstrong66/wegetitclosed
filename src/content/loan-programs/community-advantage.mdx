## What Is the Community Advantage Program?

Community Advantage is a mortgage program designed to help moderate-income borrowers achieve homeownership. It may offer more flexible qualifying guidelines than standard conventional loans, which can benefit buyers who have steady income but do not fit the typical conforming loan profile.

Program features and eligibility requirements vary by lender and program structure. Talk with a loan officer about whether Community Advantage or a similar affordable homeownership program may fit your situation.

## Who May Benefit from This Program

Community Advantage-type programs are generally designed for buyers who:

- Have moderate household income relative to area median income (AMI)
- Are purchasing a primary residence
- May have limited down payment savings
- Have stable employment but non-traditional income documentation needs
- Are looking for alternatives to standard FHA or conventional programs

Eligibility is based on income limits, credit guidelines, property type, and lender requirements. Not all borrowers will qualify.

## Potential Program Features

Depending on the specific program structure available at the time of application, Community Advantage-type programs may include:

- **Flexible income limits:** Income thresholds based on area median income
- **Low down payment options:** Down payment requirements may be lower than standard conventional loans
- **Reduced or no PMI options:** Some program structures may offer mortgage insurance alternatives
- **Down payment assistance compatibility:** May be paired with eligible down payment assistance programs

Features are subject to program availability and lender guidelines. Talk with a loan officer for current terms.

## Property Requirements

- Primary residence only
- Single-family homes, condominiums, and certain multi-unit properties
- Must meet standard property condition and appraisal requirements
- Located in AL, FL, GA, LA, MI, or TN for American Mortgage Services lending

## How It Compares to FHA

Community Advantage programs differ from FHA loans in several ways. FHA loans have government backing and set mortgage insurance premiums. Community Advantage programs may offer different mortgage insurance structures or income-based pricing. For moderate-income buyers, comparing both options with a loan officer can help identify the better fit based on your credit, income, down payment, and long-term goals.
