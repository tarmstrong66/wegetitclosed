## Why real estate investors choose DSCR loans

DSCR loans are designed with investors in mind. They remove the hurdles of conventional financing and focus entirely on property performance. Benefits include:

- No income or job history required
- Qualification based on property cash flow only
- Loan amounts up to $5 million
- Down payments starting at 20%
- Short-term rental income (Airbnb) allowed
- Interest-only payment options available
- Close under an LLC or corporation
- Unlimited cash-out refinancing
- Credit scores starting at 640

This makes DSCR loans one of the most accessible and flexible financing tools for real estate investors in Florida.

## Step 1: Understand your DSCR ratio

Your DSCR ratio determines whether you qualify for a loan and what terms you may receive. The higher the ratio, the stronger your property's financial performance.

- A DSCR of 1.0 means your property earns just enough to cover the loan payment.
- Most lenders prefer a DSCR between 1.1 and 1.25 for better stability.
- If your DSCR is below 1.0, it means your property does not fully cover its debt payments, but there are strategies to improve it before applying.

### Example DSCR calculation

Suppose your rental property earns $36,000 per year and your annual loan payments total $30,000.

**DSCR = $36,000 / $30,000 = 1.2**

That means your property produces 20% more income than needed to pay the loan, typically considered a healthy DSCR for approval.

## Step 2: Improve your DSCR before applying

A strong DSCR helps you qualify for better terms. Here are several ways to improve your ratio before applying:

- **Increase rental income:** Adjust rent or upgrade the property to attract higher-paying tenants.
- **Reduce expenses:** Minimize maintenance, insurance, or management costs to increase net operating income.
- **Make a larger down payment:** This lowers your loan amount and improves your DSCR automatically.
- **Choose a high-demand location:** Properties with low vacancy rates generate steadier income and improve your financial profile.
- **Work with a DSCR expert:** Our team can help you evaluate your property and identify ways to strengthen your DSCR before submitting your application.

## Step 3: Prepare the required documentation

Even though DSCR loans require less paperwork than traditional mortgages, you still need documentation to verify property performance. Common requirements include:

- Lease agreements or rent rolls showing current rental income
- Property appraisal or market valuation
- Bank statements for reserves or cash flow proof
- Purchase contract for new acquisitions
- LLC or corporation documents if applying under a business entity

American Mortgage Services helps you organize this information quickly so your application process runs smoothly from start to finish.

## Step 4: Maintain a strong credit profile

While DSCR loans prioritize property income, credit still matters. Most lenders require a minimum credit score of 640 or higher. A better score can help you qualify for lower rates and better loan terms.

Before applying, review your credit report, pay down existing debts, and avoid opening new accounts to keep your credit profile strong.
